Understanding the CMA Report

A Credit Monitoring Arrangement (CMA) report is a comprehensive analysis of a company's past and future financial health. It is the most critical document required by Banks and Financial Institutions to evaluate your eligibility for working capital or long-term loans.

Past Performance

Detailed analysis of the last 2 years of audited financial statements to establish credibility.

Future Projections

Realistic 3 to 5-year financial forecasting based on market trends and business capacity.

MPBF Calculation

Determining the Maximum Permissible Bank Finance to ensure you get the right amount of funding.

Key Financial Indicators We Cover

Banks approve loans based on these crucial ratios. We ensure your CMA report meets the required benchmarks.

Current Ratio

Liquidity analysis to prove you can pay short-term debts.

DSCR

Debt Service Coverage Ratio to show loan repayment capacity.

Debt-Equity

Analyzing the balance between owned capital and borrowed funds.

BEP

Break-Even Point analysis for new projects.
